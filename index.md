<!-- # The Predictive Modeling in Medicine Lab -->
The Predictive Modeling in Medicine Lab was established in January 2025 at The University of Texas at Austin by Professor Charles A. Taylor, the W. A. “Tex” Moncrief Jr. Chair in Computational Medicine at the [Oden Institute for Computational Engineering and Sciences](https://oden.utexas.edu/) and a professor in the Department of Internal Medicine at [Dell Medical School](https://dellmed.utexas.edu/). The lab is part of the [Center for Computational Medicine](https://oden.utexas.edu/research/centers-and-groups/center-for-computational-medicine/), jointly founded by the Oden Institute and Dell Medical School.

Our mission is to provide clinic-ready tools that integrate medical imaging-based structure and function models of the heart and lungs with genomic and other multi-modal data. We seek not only to improve diagnostic capabilities,  but to help doctors determine a patient's risk for developing heart and lung disease and how to best prevent further development or progression of cardiovascular or pulmonary conditions.
