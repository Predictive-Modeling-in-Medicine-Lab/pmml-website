Dr. Michael G. Kapteyn is a Research Associate in the Predictive Modeling in Medicine Lab at the Center for Computational Medicine. His work focuses on developing systems and software to enable practical medical digital twins at scale.

He has worked across academia and industry on a range of modeling and simulation projects, with applications spanning unmanned aerial vehicles (fixed-wing and rotorcraft), ocean energy turbines, and space systems. Dr. Kapteyn introduced a novel probabilistic modeling framework for predictive digital twins and applied it to the concept of a self-aware unmanned aerial vehicle capable of detecting changes in structural health and adapting its behavior accordingly. In industry, he has worked at startups developing software for engineering simulation and multidisciplinary design, contributing to product management, software engineering, and technical research. Building on this foundation, Dr. Kapteyn now applies his expertise in predictive modeling and digital twins to challenges in medicine.

Dr. Kapteyn holds a B.E. (Hons) in Engineering Science from the University of Auckland, New Zealand, an S.M. in Aeronautics and Astronautics from MIT, and a Ph.D. in Computational Science and Engineering from MIT. He completed his doctoral work with Professor Karen Willcox, focusing on mathematical and computational foundations of predictive digital twins.
