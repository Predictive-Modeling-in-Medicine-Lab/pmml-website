Kenan is a PhD Student in the Department of Biomedical Engineering at the University of Texas at Austin. He joined the lab in June 2025 after completing his undergrgaduate in the same department at UT earlier in the spring semester. His research deals with noninvasive identification of high-risk lesions for improved risk assessment for coronary artery disease (CAD), with an emphasis on identifying high-risk plaque features like thin-cap fibroatheroma (TCFA) using coronary computed tomography angiography (CCTA). Kenan leverages image processing techniques across invasive (IVUS, OCT) and noninvasive (CCTA) coronary imaging modalities with the aim of developing effective risk models for CAD.
